The Ashen Convergence

Heimr was not born; it was rendered. In the age of the Handy Protocol, the world existed as a perfect technical drafting paper grid. But an industrial overclocking—a desperate reach for "Unrivaled Innovation"—fractured the logic-brains of the foundation.

The grid lines bled. The drafting paper charred into the Iron Barrens. What remains is a Viking Gothic landscape where the circuitry of the old world pulses beneath the wet, sharp stone of the new.

Parasitic Vitality

The Sower is the architect of survival. Using Deep Emerald and Electric Blue energy, they stitch together the broken world. They don't plant life; they graft it. Thick, oily, vein-like roots snake through the Hallowed Plots, refining biological essence into the materials needed for Bio-Armor.

To be a Sower is to manage the "Plumbing" of growth, ensuring the harvest is ready before the decay sets in.

The Cold Iron Contract

Where the Sower builds, the Reaper harvests. Armed with tools stamped at the Sanguine Forge, Reapers venture into the blighted zones to collect Soul Essence and the ultra-rare Cold Iron. Every kill is a transaction; every drop of blood is a physical currency stored in Blood Vials.

Without the Reaper, the economy of Hjärta starves. Without the Sower, the Reaper has no gear to shield them from the Blight.

The Soul-Blight Protocol

Entropy is the only law in Heimr. As gear is used, it succumbs to Soul-Blight. The vibrant colors desaturate toward Brushed Silver (#C0C0C0), and textures begin to crack and ash. When a piece of equipment is fully blighted, it suffers an 80% stat penalty.

Operators must utilize Empty Vials and Refined Essence to restore their gear, or cast it into the Maw to reclaim a fraction of the Cold Iron within.
